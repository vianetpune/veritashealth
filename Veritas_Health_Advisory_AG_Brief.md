### PAGE 5: PATIENT RIGHTS IN INDIA

**SEO Title:** Know Your Rights as a Patient in India | Veritas Health Advisory  
**Meta Description:** A practical guide to patient rights in India — second opinions, medical records, informed consent, and billing transparency.

**URL:** /patient-rights

**SEO keywords this page targets:** patient rights India, right to second opinion India, medical records India, informed consent India, hospital billing transparency India

---

**INTRO:**

Headline: `Your Rights as a Patient in India`

> India's healthcare system can feel overwhelming — but patients have more rights than many realise. Understanding them is the first step to exercising them.

---

**RIGHT 1: The Right to a Second Opinion**

> No ethical doctor or hospital should discourage you from seeking a second opinion. In fact, for serious diagnoses or major procedures, a second opinion is not just your right — it is standard good practice worldwide.
>
> You are entitled to your medical records for this purpose. See below.

---

**RIGHT 2: The Right to Your Medical Records**

> You are legally entitled to copies of all your medical records, test results, imaging, and clinical notes. Hospitals are required to provide these.
>
> How to request them:
> - Submit a written request to the hospital's medical records department
> - Reference your right under the Clinical Establishments Act and MCI guidelines
> - Ask for copies of all investigation reports, discharge summaries, and imaging

---

**RIGHT 3: The Right to Informed Consent**

> Before any procedure, you have the right to:
> - A clear explanation of what will be done
> - The risks and benefits
> - The alternatives available
> - Time to decide — you are never required to consent immediately

> A consent form signed under pressure, or without understanding, is not true informed consent.

---

**RIGHT 4: The Right to Billing Transparency**

> You are entitled to an itemised bill from any hospital — a detailed breakdown of every charge. If you receive a bill that seems unclear or unusually high, you can:
> - Request a full itemised breakdown
> - Ask the hospital billing department to explain each charge
> - Seek independent guidance on whether the charges are appropriate

---

**RIGHT 5: What to Do If Something Goes Wrong**

> If you believe you have received substandard care or have been charged unfairly:
> - File a complaint with the State Medical Council
> - Approach the Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum (consumer courts handle medical negligence)
> - Seek advice from a medico-legal advisor

> Note: Veritas Health Advisory provides medical advisory services only and does not provide legal advice or representation.
