The Patient Rights CHARTER

In India, patients are protected by a "Charter of Patient Rights" issued by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) and the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC). Know what you are entitled to.

01. Right to Information & Records

Patients have the right to receive full information about their diagnosis, risks of treatment, and access to all medical records and reports within 24–72 hours of request.

Veritas Advice: If a hospital tells you that you "cannot have the records until discharge," this is incorrect under Indian law. You are entitled to them immediately.

02. Right to Transparent Pricing

Hospitals are required to provide pre-treatment cost estimates. They must display the rates of all their services and procedures in a prominent place.

Veritas Advice: Always ask for the "Detailed Estimate" rather than just a total package price to understand which services are variable.

03. Right to Informed Consent

Before any surgery, invasive procedure, or research project, doctors must explain the procedure, potential benefits, and risks to the patient or their family in a language they understand.

Blank consent forms are illegal. You should only sign after seeing the specific details of the surgery filled in.

04. Right to Second Opinion

Patients have the full right to seek a second opinion from a doctor of their choice, and the primary doctor must cooperate by providing necessary records.

Veritas Advice: Do not feel hesitant to tell your primary physician you are engaging an independent advisor. Quality doctors welcome independent peer review.

05. Right to Discharge

A hospital cannot detain a patient against their will due to a dispute over payment. While they can pursue legal action for the debt, the patient has a right to leave.
